## Summary

A **character** is a generating function that counts states in a chiral representation by their $L_0$ eigenvalue. For a chiral module $\mathcal V_i$ of central charge $c$, the character is

$$
\chi_i(\tau)=\operatorname{Tr}_{\mathcal V_i}q^{L_0-c/24},
\qquad
q=e^{2\pi i\tau}.
$$

The shift by $-c/24$ is the cylinder vacuum-energy shift. It is the same shift that appears in the torus partition function and in the plane–cylinder map.

Characters do three jobs. They count descendants, package null-state quotients, and transform linearly under modular transformations. In a rational CFT, the finite vector of characters is one of the main organizing objects:

$$
\chi_i(-1/\tau)=\sum_j S_{ij}\chi_j(\tau),
\qquad
\chi_i(\tau+1)=\sum_j T_{ij}\chi_j(\tau).
$$

This page explains the character of a Verma module, how minimal-model characters subtract null submodules, how to read a $q$-series, and why characters are the bridge from Virasoro representation theory to modular-invariant partition functions.

$$
h+N
$$

if the primary has weight $h$.

## Core idea

A chiral module is usually infinite-dimensional. Instead of listing every descendant, we record the number of states at each level in a generating function.

For a highest-weight module with primary weight $h$, the character has the form

$$
\chi_h(q)=q^{h-c/24}\sum_{N\ge0}d_N q^N,
$$

where $d_N$ is the number of independent states at level $N$ after null states have been removed.

The character is therefore a graded dimension. It does not know all inner products or OPE coefficients, but it knows the spectrum of chiral conformal energies inside the module.

The slogan is

$$
\text{module}
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
\text{graded state count}
\quad\longrightarrow\quad
\text{character}.
$$

In rational CFT, there are finitely many irreducible characters, and they transform among themselves under the modular group. This is why characters are more than bookkeeping: they are the raw material for modular invariance and the Verlinde formula.

## Setup and conventions

The torus modular parameter is

$$
\tau\in\mathbb H,
\qquad
q=e^{2\pi i\tau}.
$$

The chiral Hamiltonian on the spatial circle is

$$
L_0-\frac{c}{24}.
$$

Therefore the chiral character is

$$
\chi_i(\tau)=\operatorname{Tr}_{\mathcal V_i}q^{L_0-c/24}.
$$

For a full diagonal local theory, the torus partition function often takes the simple form

$$
Z(\tau,\bar\tau)=\sum_i |\chi_i(\tau)|^2.
$$

More generally,

$$
Z(\tau,\bar\tau)=\sum_{i,j}M_{ij}\chi_i(\tau)\overline{\chi_j(\tau)},
$$

where $M_{ij}$ is a nonnegative integer matrix specifying the left-right pairing.

## Verma module character

A Verma module is freely generated by the lowering modes

$$
L_{-n},
\qquad
n=1,2,3,\ldots .
$$

At level $N$, a basis before imposing null relations is labeled by partitions of $N$:

$$
L_{-n_1}L_{-n_2}\cdots L_{-n_k}|h\rangle,
\qquad
n_1+\cdots+n_k=N.
$$

The number of such states is the partition number $p(N)$. The generating function for partitions is

$$
\sum_{N\ge0}p(N)q^N=\prod_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{1-q^n}.
$$

Thus the Verma character is

$$
\chi_h^{\rm Verma}(q)
=\frac{q^{h-c/24}}{\prod_{n=1}^\infty(1-q^n)}.
$$

Using the Dedekind eta function

$$
\eta(\tau)=q^{1/24}\prod_{n=1}^\infty(1-q^n),
$$

this can be written as

$$
\chi_h^{\rm Verma}(\tau)=\frac{q^{h-(c-1)/24}}{\eta(\tau)}.
$$

This formula counts too many states whenever the Verma module has null descendants. Minimal-model characters must subtract null submodules.

## Vacuum character

The vacuum module is not a generic Verma module. In a local CFT, translation of the identity vanishes:

$$
L_{-1}|0\rangle=0.
$$

Therefore the vacuum character has no level-one state. If there are no additional null vectors, the Virasoro vacuum character is

$$
\chi_{\rm vac}(q)
=q^{-c/24}\prod_{n=2}^\infty\frac{1}{1-q^n}.
$$

The missing $n=1$ oscillator is important. Forgetting it is one of the quickest ways to get the wrong torus partition function or the wrong low-level state count.

In minimal models, the vacuum module has further null states, so the final irreducible vacuum character is smaller than this generic vacuum expression.

## Minimal-model characters

For the minimal model $\mathcal M(p,p')$, the central charge is

$$
c=1-\frac{6(p'-p)^2}{pp'}.
$$

The Kac labels are

$$
1\le r\le p-1,
\qquad
1\le s\le p'-1,
\qquad
(r,s)\sim(p-r,p'-s).
$$

The irreducible minimal-model characters are given by the Rocha–Caridi formula:

$$
\chi_{r,s}^{(p,p')}(\tau)
=\frac{1}{\eta(\tau)}
\sum_{n\in\mathbb Z}
\left(
q^{(2pp'n+p'r-ps)^2/(4pp')}
-q^{(2pp'n+p'r+ps)^2/(4pp')}
\right).
$$

This formula is a compact way of performing the infinite subtraction of null submodules. The first term counts a tower of states; the second term subtracts the null tower; the repeated sum implements the alternating structure of embedded submodules.

The field identification is visible at the character level:

$$
\chi_{r,s}^{(p,p')}=\chi_{p-r,p'-s}^{(p,p')}.
$$

## Example: Ising characters

The Ising CFT is $\mathcal M(3,4)$ with

$$
c=\frac12.
$$

It has three irreducible Virasoro characters:

$$
\chi_0(\tau),
\qquad
\chi_{1/2}(\tau),
\qquad
\chi_{1/16}(\tau).
$$

Their first terms are

$$
\chi_0(q)=q^{-1/48}\left(1+q^2+q^3+2q^4+2q^5+\cdots\right),
$$

$$
\chi_{1/2}(q)=q^{23/48}\left(1+q+q^2+2q^3+2q^4+\cdots\right),
$$

and

$$
\chi_{1/16}(q)=q^{1/24}\left(1+q+q^2+2q^3+3q^4+\cdots\right).
$$

The exponents are $h-c/24$. For example,

$$
\frac{1}{16}-\frac{1}{48}=\frac{1}{24}.
$$

The diagonal Ising partition function is

$$
Z_{\rm Ising}(\tau,\bar\tau)
=|\chi_0|^2+|\chi_{1/2}|^2+|\chi_{1/16}|^2.
$$

This expression is much more than a sum of functions. It says that the full local bosonic Ising CFT pairs each chiral representation with the same antiholomorphic representation.

## Example: tricritical Ising characters

The tricritical Ising CFT is $\mathcal M(4,5)$ with

$$
c=\frac{7}{10}.
$$

Its six diagonal primary weights are

$$
0,\quad \frac{1}{10},\quad \frac{3}{5},\quad \frac{3}{2},\quad \frac{3}{80},\quad \frac{7}{16}.
$$

The diagonal partition function is

$$
Z_{\rm TCI}(\tau,\bar\tau)
=\sum_{h\in\{0,1/10,3/5,3/2,3/80,7/16\}}|\chi_h(\tau)|^2.
$$

Each character contains infinitely many descendants, but there are only six irreducible Virasoro families. This is the character-level meaning of rationality for the tricritical Ising model.

## Reading a character

Suppose

$$
\chi_i(q)=q^{h_i-c/24}\left(d_0+d_1q+d_2q^2+\cdots\right).
$$

Then:

| Coefficient | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| $d_0$ | Number of independent primary states in the representation, usually $1$. |
| $d_1$ | Number of independent level-one descendants after null relations. |
| $d_2$ | Number of independent level-two descendants after null relations. |
| $d_N$ | Number of independent level-$N$ states. |

A null state reduces these coefficients relative to the Verma-module partition numbers. For instance, a level-two null vector removes a state at level two and also removes all descendants of that null state at higher levels.

Thus character coefficients are a quick diagnostic of representation structure.

## Modular transformation behavior

Characters in a rational CFT transform in a finite-dimensional way under

$$
S:\tau\mapsto-\frac1\tau,
\qquad
T:\tau\mapsto\tau+1.
$$

The $T$ transformation is diagonal in a basis of irreducible characters:

$$
\chi_i(\tau+1)=e^{2\pi i(h_i-c/24)}\chi_i(\tau).
$$

The $S$ transformation is more interesting:

$$
\chi_i(-1/\tau)=\sum_j S_{ij}\chi_j(\tau).
$$

For the Ising CFT, in the basis $(\chi_0,\chi_{1/2},\chi_{1/16})$, the $S$ matrix is

$$
S=\frac12
\begin{pmatrix}
1&1&\sqrt2\\
1&1&-\sqrt2\\
\sqrt2&-\sqrt2&0
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

This matrix encodes quantum dimensions and fusion rules through the Verlinde formula. For example,

$$
d_i=\frac{S_{i0}}{S_{00}},
$$

so

$$
d_{\mathbf 1}=1,
\qquad
d_\epsilon=1,
\qquad
d_\sigma=\sqrt2.
$$

## Character identities and theta functions

Many familiar rational-CFT characters can be written in terms of theta functions and the eta function. For example, the Ising characters can be expressed as combinations of square roots of theta-function ratios:

$$
\chi_0=\frac12\left(\sqrt{\frac{\vartheta_3}{\eta}}+\sqrt{\frac{\vartheta_4}{\eta}}\right),
$$

$$
\chi_{1/2}=\frac12\left(\sqrt{\frac{\vartheta_3}{\eta}}-\sqrt{\frac{\vartheta_4}{\eta}}\right),
$$

and

$$
\chi_{1/16}=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\sqrt{\frac{\vartheta_2}{\eta}}.
$$

These formulas are useful for modular transformations because $\eta$ and $\vartheta_i$ have simple modular behavior. They also reveal the free-fermion origin of the Ising model.

For learning minimal models, however, the Rocha–Caridi formula is usually the cleaner starting point, because it displays the null-state subtraction directly.

## What characters do not know

Characters are powerful but incomplete. They do not determine the full CFT by themselves.

| Characters know | Characters do not know by themselves |
|---|---|
| Chiral state counts | All OPE coefficients |
| Conformal weights modulo integers | Full local operator algebra |
| Modular transformation representation | Which modular invariant is chosen |
| Null-state quotient structure | Full correlator normalization |
| Asymptotic state growth | Locality and crossing data by themselves |

Two theories can share character-like data but differ in left-right pairing, extended algebra interpretation, or local operator products. Characters are a gateway to rational CFT, not the whole house.

## Common pitfalls

**Do not forget the $-c/24$ shift.** Characters on the torus count cylinder energies, not plane weights alone.

**Do not use the Verma character after quotienting.** Minimal-model irreducible characters subtract null submodules.

**Do not confuse a character with a partition function.** A character is chiral. A local torus partition function pairs holomorphic and antiholomorphic characters.

**Do not assume diagonal pairing.** Diagonal partition functions are common, but non-diagonal modular invariants are central in rational CFT.

**Do not say rational CFT has finitely many states.** Each character contains infinitely many descendants. Rationality means finitely many irreducible chiral families.

**Do not treat $q$ as a small formal variable only.** The $q$-series is a formal counting device, but on the torus $q=e^{2\pi i\tau}$ also has geometric meaning.

## Research status

Virasoro and rational-CFT characters are established technology. Minimal-model characters, modular transformations, and their role in torus partition functions are standard parts of two-dimensional CFT.

Active directions include logarithmic characters, mock modular behavior, supersymmetric indices, vertex-operator-algebra character classification, modular tensor categories, and the use of modular constraints in modern bootstrap problems.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1

Show that the generic Verma-module character is

$$
\chi_h^{\rm Verma}(q)
=q^{h-c/24}\prod_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{1-q^n}.
$$

<details><summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

At level $N$, descendants are labeled by partitions of $N$:

$$
N=n_1+\cdots+n_k.
$$

Each mode $L_{-n}$ can appear any nonnegative number of times. Therefore the generating function is

$$
\prod_{n=1}^\infty(1+q^n+q^{2n}+\cdots)
=\prod_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{1-q^n}.
$$

Multiplying by the primary cylinder energy $q^{h-c/24}$ gives the result.

</details>

### Exercise 2

Why does the generic vacuum character start with a product over $n\ge2$ instead of $n\ge1$?

<details><summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

The state $L_{-1}|0\rangle$ corresponds to the derivative of the identity operator:

$$
\partial\mathbf 1=0.
$$

Thus the level-one vacuum descendant is null and must be removed. The remaining freely generated Virasoro descendants begin with $L_{-2}$, so the generic vacuum character is

$$
\chi_{\rm vac}(q)=q^{-c/24}\prod_{n=2}^\infty\frac{1}{1-q^n}
$$

before any additional null-state quotients.

</details>

### Exercise 3

For the Ising spin character, verify the leading power $q^{1/24}$.

<details><summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

The Ising spin field has

$$
h_\sigma=\frac{1}{16},
\qquad
c=\frac12.
$$

The leading character exponent is

$$
h_\sigma-\frac{c}{24}
=\frac{1}{16}-\frac{1}{48}
=\frac{3-1}{48}
=\frac{1}{24}.
$$

</details>

### Exercise 4

Use the Ising $S$ matrix to compute $d_\sigma$.

<details><summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

In the basis $(\mathbf 1,\epsilon,\sigma)$, the vacuum column has

$$
S_{00}=\frac12,
\qquad
S_{\sigma0}=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}.
$$

Thus

$$
d_\sigma=\frac{S_{\sigma0}}{S_{00}}=\sqrt2.
$$

</details>
