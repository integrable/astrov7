Operators, States, and Radial Quantization is the chapter where CFT starts to look unlike ordinary perturbative QFT. Instead of building the theory around asymptotic particles and scattering amplitudes, we build it around local operators, their scaling dimensions, and the Hilbert-space states they create on spheres.

The central idea is the operator–state correspondence:

$$
\mathcal O(0)|0\rangle
\quad\longleftrightarrow\quad
|\mathcal O\rangle\in\mathcal H(S^{d-1}).
$$

Radial distance plays the role of Euclidean time. Dilatations become time translations on the cylinder $\mathbb R\times S^{d-1}$, so the scaling dimension $\Delta$ of a local operator becomes the energy of the corresponding cylinder state.

Read this chapter when you want to understand why CFT data are organized by primary operators, descendants, conformal multiplets, spin, scaling dimension, positivity, and shortening. This is the representation-theory bridge between conformal symmetry and the correlation-function/OPE technology used by the bootstrap.

You should know the CFT-specific [Conventions and Notation](/cft-bootstrap/conventions/), the conceptual entrance in [What Is a CFT?](/cft-bootstrap/what-is-a-cft/), and the conformal generators in [Conformal Symmetry](/cft-bootstrap/conformal-symmetry/). The pages [Conformal Algebra](/cft-bootstrap/conformal-symmetry/conformal-algebra/) and [Conformal Generators](/cft-bootstrap/conformal-symmetry/conformal-generators/) are especially important.

It helps to remember basic Hilbert-space quantum mechanics and the idea of Euclidean time evolution. You do not need prior two-dimensional CFT; radial quantization is a general CFT idea in any dimension.

## Landmarks

| Landmark | Meaning | Where used next |
|---|---|---|
| $\mathcal O(0)|0\rangle$ | A local operator at the origin creates a state on the surrounding sphere. | State–operator correspondence, OPE, conformal blocks. |
| $D=H_{\mathrm{cyl}}$ | The dilatation generator becomes the Hamiltonian on $\mathbb R\times S^{d-1}$. | Scaling dimensions, radial quantization, thermal CFT. |
| $K_\mu|\mathcal O\rangle=0$ | A primary is a lowest-weight state of the conformal algebra. | Conformal multiplets, unitarity bounds. |
| $P_\mu|\mathcal O\rangle$ | Acting with translations creates descendants with dimension increased by one. | Conformal blocks, descendant contributions to the OPE. |
| $\Delta$ and spin | The local operator spectrum is labeled by scaling dimension and rotation representation. | Correlator selection rules and bootstrap spectra. |
| Inversion conjugation | Radial adjoints involve the map $x^\mu\mapsto x^\mu/x^2$. | Reflection positivity, inner products, unitarity bounds. |
| Null descendants | A descendant with zero norm signals a conservation law or shortening condition. | Conserved currents, stress tensor, current blocks. |

The most important formula is the cylinder map. Writing flat Euclidean space as

$$
ds^2=dr^2+r^2d\Omega_{d-1}^2,
\qquad
r=e^\tau,
$$

gives

$$
ds^2=e^{2\tau}(d\tau^2+d\Omega_{d-1}^2).
$$

A CFT is insensitive to the Weyl factor in flat-space kinematics, up to anomaly subtleties on curved backgrounds, so the natural geometry becomes the cylinder

$$
\mathbb R_\tau\times S^{d-1}.
$$

Translations in $\tau$ are dilatations in $r$. Thus a state created by an operator of dimension $\Delta$ evolves as

$$
e^{-\tau D}|\mathcal O\rangle=e^{-\tau\Delta}|\mathcal O\rangle.
$$

This is why the scaling dimension is not merely a number in a correlation function. It is an energy eigenvalue in radial quantization.

## Common confusions

**Primary does not mean elementary.** A primary operator is defined representation-theoretically: it is annihilated by the special conformal generators at the origin. It need not be a fundamental field in a Lagrangian.

**Descendant does not mean less real.** Descendants are genuine local operators, but their data are fixed by the primary and conformal symmetry. They are not independent CFT data in the same way primaries are.

**Scaling dimension is not always engineering dimension.** At an interacting fixed point, $\Delta$ includes anomalous contributions. Engineering dimension is a useful UV or free-field guide, not the final CFT spectrum.

**Radial quantization is not only two-dimensional.** Two-dimensional CFT makes radial quantization visually simple using circles in the complex plane, but the construction works in any dimension using spheres $S^{d-1}$.

**Cylinder energy is not the original flat-space Hamiltonian.** The cylinder Hamiltonian is the dilatation generator. It is the natural Hamiltonian for CFT representation theory, not the time-translation generator of Lorentzian flat space.

**Hermitian conjugation is geometric.** In radial quantization, adjoints involve inversion. This is why inner products are computed by putting conjugate operators at inverted positions, not merely by complex conjugating coefficients.

**Conservation means shortening.** A conserved current is not just an operator with an extra equation. In representation language, the conservation equation sets a descendant to zero, shortening the conformal multiplet.

## Boundaries

This chapter is the home of operator and Hilbert-space structure in CFT: primaries, descendants, multiplets, radial quantization, the cylinder picture, inner products, unitarity bounds, and shortening.

It does not derive the full forms of two-point, three-point, and four-point functions; that belongs to [Correlation Functions](/cft-bootstrap/correlation-functions/). It does not develop the OPE as an operator algebra; that belongs to [Operator Product Expansion](/cft-bootstrap/operator-product-expansion/). It does not compute conformal blocks in detail; that belongs to [Conformal Blocks](/cft-bootstrap/conformal-blocks/).

The chapter also does not replace the two-dimensional Virasoro construction. In two dimensions, every global conformal multiplet can further organize into Virasoro descendants when the stress tensor modes are included. That stronger structure belongs to [Two-Dimensional CFT](/cft-bootstrap/two-dimensional-cft/) and [Virasoro Symmetry and Central Charge](/cft-bootstrap/virasoro-central-charge/).

Finally, this chapter treats local operators. Boundary operators, defect operators, line operators, and extended objects have related but modified state–operator maps. Those belong to [Boundary, Defect, and Interface CFT](/cft-bootstrap/boundary-defect-interface-cft/) and the broader symmetry volumes.

## Where to go next

After this chapter, read [Correlation Functions](/cft-bootstrap/correlation-functions/). The operator labels introduced here become the labels that appear in two-point functions, three-point coefficients, and four-point decompositions.

Then read [Operator Product Expansion](/cft-bootstrap/operator-product-expansion/). Radial quantization turns the OPE into a convergent expansion of states, which is why CFT can treat the OPE as a powerful operator algebra rather than a purely asymptotic short-distance mnemonic.

For the bootstrap route, continue to [Conformal Blocks](/cft-bootstrap/conformal-blocks/) and [Crossing Symmetry and Bootstrap Logic](/cft-bootstrap/crossing-bootstrap-logic/). Conformal blocks are precisely the organized contribution of one primary and all of its descendants to a four-point function.
