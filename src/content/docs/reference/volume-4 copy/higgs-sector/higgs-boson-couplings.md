## Summary

In the minimal Standard Model, the same Higgs vacuum that gives particles mass also fixes their tree-level couplings to the physical Higgs boson. In unitary gauge,

$$
H(x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\begin{pmatrix}
0\\ v+h(x)
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The gauge-boson mass terms are therefore promoted schematically as

$$
v^2\quad\longrightarrow\quad (v+h)^2.
$$

More explicitly,

$$
\mathcal L\supset
m_W^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}\left(1+\frac{h}{v}\right)^2
+
\frac12m_Z^2Z_\mu Z^\mu\left(1+\frac{h}{v}\right)^2.
$$

Thus the tree-level Higgs couplings to massive vector bosons are

$$
hW^+W^-:\quad i\frac{2m_W^2}{v}\eta_{\mu\nu},
\qquad
hZZ:\quad i\frac{2m_Z^2}{v}\eta_{\mu\nu},
$$

and

$$
hhW^+W^-:\quad i\frac{2m_W^2}{v^2}\eta_{\mu\nu},
\qquad
hhZZ:\quad i\frac{2m_Z^2}{v^2}\eta_{\mu\nu}.
$$

For a charged fermion mass eigenstate $f$,

$$
\mathcal L\supset -m_f\bar f f-\frac{m_f}{v}h\bar f f,
$$

so the tree-level Yukawa vertex is

$$
h\bar f f:\quad -i\frac{m_f}{v}.
$$

The Higgs self-interactions follow from the potential,

$$
V(h)=
\frac12m_h^2h^2
+\frac{m_h^2}{2v}h^3
+\frac{m_h^2}{8v^2}h^4,
$$

so, with all momenta incoming,

$$
hhh:\quad -i\frac{3m_h^2}{v},
\qquad
hhhh:\quad -i\frac{3m_h^2}{v^2}.
$$

## Prerequisites

You should know [Gauge Boson Masses](/gauge-theories-standard-model/higgs-sector/gauge-boson-masses/) and [Higgs Potential](/gauge-theories-standard-model/higgs-sector/higgs-potential/). You should also know the Yukawa-coupling setup from [Standard Model Higgs Doublet](/gauge-theories-standard-model/higgs-sector/standard-model-higgs-doublet/), at least at the level that $H$ and $\widetilde H=i\sigma^2H^\ast$ allow different gauge-invariant fermion mass terms.

This page is a tree-level coupling page. It does not compute Higgs decay rates, electroweak radiative corrections, loop-induced form factors, or global coupling fits.

## Core idea

The physical Higgs field $h$ is the fluctuation in the magnitude of the electroweak order parameter. Coupling one external Higgs boson to a massive particle asks: how does that particle's mass term change when the vacuum scale is locally shifted?

For a mass generated by the Higgs vacuum,

$$
v\mapsto v+h(x)
$$

is the guiding replacement. For vector bosons,

$$
m_V^2\propto v^2,
$$

so one Higgs insertion gives a coupling proportional to

$$
\frac{\partial m_V^2}{\partial v}=\frac{2m_V^2}{v}.
$$

For fermions,

$$
m_f\propto v,
$$

so one Higgs insertion gives

$$
\frac{\partial m_f}{\partial v}=\frac{m_f}{v}.
$$

This is the origin of the famous mass-proportional Higgs couplings. It is not a mysterious new force added by hand; it is the fluctuation of the field that made the mass term gauge invariant in the first place.

## Conventions for vertices

The formulas below use the mostly-minus metric convention of this volume and the interaction rule

$$
\text{vertex factor}=i\times
\text{coefficient obtained by differentiating } \mathcal L_{\rm int}
\text{ with respect to the fields}.
$$

All momenta are taken incoming in the displayed Feynman rules. The metric factor for vector vertices is $\eta_{\mu\nu}$.

For identical fields, combinatorial factors matter. For example, if

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm int}\supset -\frac{\lambda_3}{3!}h^3,
$$

then the $hhh$ vertex is $-i\lambda_3$. If instead the Lagrangian contains $-ah^3$, the vertex is $-i\,3!a$.

This page gives tree-level Standard Model vertices. Gauge-dependent Goldstone and ghost couplings in $R_\xi$ gauges are not listed here.

## Couplings to W and Z bosons

Start with the Higgs kinetic term in unitary gauge,

$$
H=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\begin{pmatrix}
0\\v+h
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The gauge-boson mass part of $(D_\mu H)^\dagger(D^\mu H)$ becomes

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm vector}
=
\frac{g^2(v+h)^2}{4}W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
+
\frac{(g^2+g'^2)(v+h)^2}{8}Z_\mu Z^\mu.
$$

Using

$$
m_W^2=\frac{g^2v^2}{4},
\qquad
m_Z^2=\frac{(g^2+g'^2)v^2}{4},
$$

this is

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm vector}
=
m_W^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
\left(1+\frac{h}{v}\right)^2
+
\frac12m_Z^2Z_\mu Z^\mu
\left(1+\frac{h}{v}\right)^2.
$$

Expanding,

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm vector}
\supset
m_W^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
+
\frac{2m_W^2}{v}hW_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
+
\frac{m_W^2}{v^2}h^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
$$

and

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm vector}
\supset
\frac12m_Z^2Z_\mu Z^\mu
+
\frac{m_Z^2}{v}hZ_\mu Z^\mu
+
\frac{m_Z^2}{2v^2}h^2Z_\mu Z^\mu.
$$

The corresponding vertices are

| vertex | tree-level rule |
|---|---|
| $hW_\mu^+W_\nu^-$ | $i\dfrac{2m_W^2}{v}\eta_{\mu\nu}$ |
| $hhW_\mu^+W_\nu^-$ | $i\dfrac{2m_W^2}{v^2}\eta_{\mu\nu}$ |
| $hZ_\mu Z_\nu$ | $i\dfrac{2m_Z^2}{v}\eta_{\mu\nu}$ |
| $hhZ_\mu Z_\nu$ | $i\dfrac{2m_Z^2}{v^2}\eta_{\mu\nu}$ |

The $Z$ vertices get the same final factors as the $W$ vertices because the fields are identical: differentiating with respect to two $Z$ fields supplies a factor of $2$.

There is no tree-level $h\gamma\gamma$ or $hZ\gamma$ coupling in the minimal renormalizable Standard Model. Those couplings arise at loop level or through higher-dimensional operators.

## Couplings to fermions

The charged-fermion Yukawa terms are

$$
\mathcal L_Y
=
-\bar Q_LY_dHd_R
-\bar Q_LY_u\widetilde H u_R
-\bar L_LY_eHe_R
+\text{h.c.},
$$

where

$$
\widetilde H=i\sigma^2H^\ast.
$$

In unitary gauge,

$$
H=
\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\begin{pmatrix}0\\v+h\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\widetilde H=
\frac{1}{\sqrt2}
\begin{pmatrix}v+h\\0\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Thus the Yukawa terms become

$$
\mathcal L_Y
\supset
-\frac{v+h}{\sqrt2}\bar d_LY_dd_R
-\frac{v+h}{\sqrt2}\bar u_LY_uu_R
-\frac{v+h}{\sqrt2}\bar e_LY_ee_R
+\text{h.c.}
$$

The mass matrices are

$$
M_d=\frac{v}{\sqrt2}Y_d,
\qquad
M_u=\frac{v}{\sqrt2}Y_u,
\qquad
M_e=\frac{v}{\sqrt2}Y_e.
$$

After diagonalizing the mass matrices, a charged fermion mass eigenstate has

$$
\mathcal L_Y
\supset
-m_f\bar f f
-\frac{m_f}{v}h\bar f f.
$$

Therefore the tree-level Higgs–fermion vertex is

$$
h\bar f f:\quad -i\frac{m_f}{v}.
$$

Equivalently, the dimensionless Yukawa coupling for a diagonal mass eigenstate is

$$
y_f=\frac{\sqrt2m_f}{v}.
$$

Gauge symmetry determines which Yukawa matrices are allowed, but it does not determine their numerical entries. The enormous spread of charged-fermion masses is therefore encoded in the Yukawa matrices, not predicted by the minimal Standard Model.

## Flavor and basis dependence

Before diagonalizing Yukawa matrices, the Higgs couplings are matrix-valued:

$$
\mathcal L_Y
\supset
-\bar f_LM_ff_R
-\frac{h}{v}\bar f_LM_ff_R
+\text{h.c.}
$$

For one fermion species at a time, the same biunitary rotations that diagonalize $M_f$ also diagonalize the neutral Higgs coupling. Therefore, in the minimal Standard Model, the physical Higgs has no tree-level flavor-changing neutral couplings.

Flavor mixing appears in charged currents because the up-type and down-type Yukawa matrices are diagonalized by different left-handed rotations. That story belongs to the [Flavor and Neutrinos](/gauge-theories-standard-model/flavor-neutrinos/) chapter. The Higgs-sector moral is simple: neutral Higgs couplings are aligned with fermion mass matrices in the minimal one-doublet Standard Model.

## Higgs self-couplings

From the Higgs potential page,

$$
V(h)=
\frac12m_h^2h^2
+\frac{m_h^2}{2v}h^3
+\frac{m_h^2}{8v^2}h^4.
$$

Since $\mathcal L$ contains $-V$, the interaction terms are

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm self}
\supset
-\frac{m_h^2}{2v}h^3
-\frac{m_h^2}{8v^2}h^4.
$$

Writing

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm self}
\supset
-\frac{\lambda_3}{3!}h^3
-\frac{\lambda_4}{4!}h^4,
$$

the tree-level Standard Model values are

$$
\lambda_3=\frac{3m_h^2}{v},
\qquad
\lambda_4=\frac{3m_h^2}{v^2}.
$$

The corresponding vertices are

$$
hhh:\quad -i\lambda_3=-i\frac{3m_h^2}{v},
$$

and

$$
hhhh:\quad -i\lambda_4=-i\frac{3m_h^2}{v^2}.
$$

These self-couplings probe the shape of the Higgs potential itself, not merely the fact that the Higgs field gives masses to other particles.

## Why the photon and gluon are special

At tree level, the minimal Higgs boson does not couple directly to two photons or two gluons. The reason is structural.

Photons are massless because the Higgs vacuum preserves $U(1)_{\rm em}$. Gluons are massless because the Higgs doublet is a color singlet. There are no tree-level mass terms

$$
m_A^2A_\mu A^\mu,
\qquad
m_g^2G_\mu^aG^{a\mu},
$$

and therefore no tree-level couplings obtained by replacing $v$ with $v+h$ in such mass terms.

Nevertheless, processes such as $h\to\gamma\gamma$ and $h\to gg$ occur in the Standard Model through loops of charged or colored particles. At energies below the particles in the loop, these effects can be represented by higher-dimensional operators such as

$$
\frac{c_\gamma}{\Lambda}hF_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu},
\qquad
\frac{c_g}{\Lambda}hG_{\mu\nu}^aG^{a\mu\nu}.
$$

In a gauge-invariant SMEFT language before electroweak symmetry breaking, the corresponding operators involve $H^\dagger H$ multiplying gauge kinetic invariants. Those belong to the later SMEFT material.

## The mass-proportionality rule and its limits

At tree level in the minimal Standard Model,

$$
g_{hVV}\propto m_V^2,
\qquad
g_{hff}\propto m_f.
$$

This is one of the central experimental fingerprints of the Higgs mechanism. The physical Higgs boson couples most strongly to the heaviest particles whose masses arise from the Higgs vacuum.

But the slogan needs care.

First, most of the proton mass does not come from light-quark Yukawa masses. It comes from QCD confinement and dimensional transmutation. The Higgs couples directly to quark mass terms, not to “all mass” in some universal gravitational way.

Second, loop-induced couplings can be important even when no tree-level coupling exists. The Higgs couples to photons through loops of charged particles and to gluons primarily through heavy colored loops.

Third, beyond the minimal Standard Model, Higgs couplings can deviate from these tree-level relations. Extra scalars, mixing, compositeness, or higher-dimensional operators can all change the pattern.

The useful rule is therefore:

$$
\text{minimal tree-level Higgs coupling}
=
\text{fluctuation of the Higgs-generated mass term}.
$$

That is powerful, but not the same as saying the Higgs couples directly to every kind of energy.

## Gauge choices and Goldstone couplings

This page used unitary gauge because it displays the physical $h$, $W^\pm$, $Z$, $A$, and fermions most directly. For loop calculations, one usually works in covariant $R_\xi$ gauges. Then the would-be Goldstone fields $G^\pm$ and $G^0$ remain in the Lagrangian, and there are additional Higgs–Goldstone, gauge–Goldstone, and ghost couplings.

Those extra vertices are not optional bookkeeping errors. They are required by gauge invariance and BRST symmetry in covariant gauges. The physical S-matrix is independent of the gauge parameter, but intermediate Feynman rules are not.

[Goldstone Equivalence Theorem](/gauge-theories-standard-model/higgs-sector/goldstone-equivalence-theorem/), treated next, explains why these would-be Goldstone fields are not merely computational clutter: they control high-energy amplitudes involving longitudinal $W$ and $Z$ bosons.

## Common pitfalls

**Forgetting that $hVV$ scales like $m_V^2$, not $m_V$.** Vector masses come from quadratic terms proportional to $v^2$, so differentiating with respect to $v$ gives $2m_V^2/v$.

**Forgetting identical-particle combinatorics.** The $hZZ$ and $hhZZ$ terms in the Lagrangian look different from the $W$ terms because $Z$ is a real field and $W^\pm$ are complex conjugates. The final vertex factors are nevertheless $2m_Z^2/v$ and $2m_Z^2/v^2$ because of identical $Z$ combinatorics.

**Calling every Higgs coupling a Yukawa coupling.** Strictly, Yukawa couplings are scalar–fermion couplings. The $hWW$ and $hZZ$ couplings come from the Higgs kinetic term, not from Yukawa terms.

**Saying the Higgs couples to mass without qualification.** The tree-level Higgs coupling tracks mass generated by the Higgs vacuum. Bound-state masses and anomalous scales require a more careful field-theory statement.

**Adding tree-level $h\gamma\gamma$ by analogy with $hZZ$.** The photon is massless because $U(1)_{\rm em}$ is unbroken. The minimal Standard Model has no tree-level $h\gamma\gamma$ vertex; it is loop-induced.

**Treating Higgs self-couplings as already measured with the same precision as $hZZ$.** The tree-level formulas are definite, but experimentally constraining the self-coupling is much harder than measuring many single-Higgs couplings.

## Research status

The tree-level formulas on this page are established consequences of the minimal Standard Model. They are the baseline for Higgs phenomenology and for SMEFT analyses.

Active research and experimental work ask whether the observed Higgs couplings are exactly minimal. Common parameterizations introduce coupling modifiers, loop-sensitive effective couplings, or gauge-invariant SMEFT Wilson coefficients. The Higgs self-coupling is especially important because it probes the scalar potential directly. Loop-induced couplings such as $h\gamma\gamma$ and $hgg$ are also sensitive to heavy charged or colored states, making them precision windows into possible ultraviolet physics.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Derive the hWW and hhWW interactions

Starting from

$$
m_W^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}\left(1+\frac{h}{v}\right)^2,
$$

extract the $hW^+W^-$ and $hhW^+W^-$ interaction terms.

<details>
<summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

Expand:

$$
\left(1+\frac{h}{v}\right)^2
=
1+\frac{2h}{v}+\frac{h^2}{v^2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\mathcal L\supset
m_W^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
+
\frac{2m_W^2}{v}hW_\mu^+W^{-\mu}
+
\frac{m_W^2}{v^2}h^2W_\mu^+W^{-\mu}.
$$

The first term is the mass term. The second and third terms are the $hW^+W^-$ and $hhW^+W^-$ interactions.

</details>

### Exercise 2: Explain the factor of two in the hZZ vertex

The Lagrangian contains

$$
\mathcal L\supset \frac{m_Z^2}{v}hZ_\mu Z^\mu.
$$

Why is the vertex $i(2m_Z^2/v)\eta_{\mu\nu}$ rather than $i(m_Z^2/v)\eta_{\mu\nu}$?

<details>
<summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

The two $Z$ fields are identical. The vertex factor is obtained by differentiating the interaction term with respect to both $Z$ fields. Differentiating $Z_\mu Z^\mu$ gives a factor of $2$ and the metric tensor. Therefore

$$
hZ_\mu Z_\nu:\quad i\frac{2m_Z^2}{v}\eta_{\mu\nu}.
$$

This is the same combinatoric logic behind writing a real vector mass term as $\frac12m_Z^2Z_\mu Z^\mu$.

</details>

### Exercise 3: Derive the fermion Higgs coupling

For one charged fermion, suppose

$$
\mathcal L_Y=-y_f\bar f_LH_f f_R+\text{h.c.}
$$

reduces in unitary gauge to

$$
\mathcal L_Y=-\frac{y_f}{\sqrt2}(v+h)\bar f_Lf_R+\text{h.c.}
$$

Show that the Higgs coupling is $-m_fh\bar f f/v$.

<details>
<summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

The fermion mass is

$$
m_f=\frac{y_fv}{\sqrt2}.
$$

The $h$ term is

$$
-\frac{y_f}{\sqrt2}h\bar f_Lf_R+\text{h.c.}
=
-\frac{m_f}{v}h(\bar f_Lf_R+\bar f_Rf_L).
$$

Since

$$
\bar f f=\bar f_Lf_R+\bar f_Rf_L,
$$

we get

$$
\mathcal L\supset -\frac{m_f}{v}h\bar f f.
$$

The vertex is $-im_f/v$.

</details>

### Exercise 4: Convert the Higgs potential to vertex factors

Given

$$
V(h)\supset
\frac{m_h^2}{2v}h^3
+\frac{m_h^2}{8v^2}h^4,
$$

derive the $hhh$ and $hhhh$ vertex factors.

<details>
<summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

The Lagrangian contains

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm int}\supset
-\frac{m_h^2}{2v}h^3
-\frac{m_h^2}{8v^2}h^4.
$$

For the cubic vertex, differentiating with respect to three identical $h$ fields gives a factor $3!$:

$$
hhh:\quad
-i\,3!\frac{m_h^2}{2v}
=
-i\frac{3m_h^2}{v}.
$$

For the quartic vertex,

$$
hhhh:\quad
-i\,4!\frac{m_h^2}{8v^2}
=
-i\frac{3m_h^2}{v^2}.
$$

Equivalently, write

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm int}\supset
-\frac{\lambda_3}{3!}h^3
-\frac{\lambda_4}{4!}h^4,
$$

with

$$
\lambda_3=\frac{3m_h^2}{v},
\qquad
\lambda_4=\frac{3m_h^2}{v^2}.
$$

</details>

### Exercise 5: Why no tree-level hγγ vertex?

Use the gauge-boson mass logic to explain why there is no tree-level $h\gamma\gamma$ vertex in the minimal Standard Model.

<details>
<summary><strong>Solution</strong></summary>

Tree-level $hVV$ couplings arise by replacing $v$ with $v+h$ in vector-boson mass terms. The photon is the gauge boson of the unbroken generator $Q=T^3+Y$, so it has no mass term:

$$
m_A^2A_\mu A^\mu=0.
$$

There is therefore no mass term whose $v\mapsto v+h$ expansion could generate a tree-level $h\gamma\gamma$ vertex.

The physical process $h\to\gamma\gamma$ still exists in the Standard Model, but it is loop-induced by charged particles.

</details>
